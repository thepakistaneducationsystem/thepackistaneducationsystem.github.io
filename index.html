Education in Pakistan

Goals and Aspirations



Memorization is an important skill to have in Pakistani schools. Lessons often consist of reciting information repeatedly until it is retained.

Religion is a large influence in Pakistani education. A large number of teachers are known to reject the concept of evolution and focus on teaching religious ideas instead.

Education in Pakistan is heavily centered around standard academics. This includes core subjects such as Math, Science, History, and either a course in English or Urdu depending on the region.



Important Statistics

Approximately 44% percent of children aged 5-16 do not attend a form of schooling Pakistan, making it the second highest country in the world in terms of out-of-school children.

At grade nine, only 13% of women are enrolled in school, compared to 35% in men.

The literacy rate for students aged 15-24 is 74%, much higher than the older generations at 46%.





Extracurriculars

In Pakistan, there is a minimal focus on extra-curricular activities due to the ongoing prevalance of academic culture. Sports such as soccer, cricket, and badmington are played mininmally during recess time. Clubs and organizations are often formed outside of school, such as the Cricket committee at Karachi Grammar School (pictured left). The opportunities given in school resemble events such as field days and other festivals.




Women in Schooling



Women in Pakistan aren't given much choice in their path of education, due to societal pressures from parents, relatives, and even neighbors. Large disparities within gender are caused by the unsafe conditions in certain regions, along with the ongoing practice of child marriage. At grade six, only 41% of women attend school, compared to 51% of men. This drops down to an abysmal 13% by ninth grade. Activists such as Malala Yousafzai have been campaiging to make change at a legislative level, with efforts being supported across the globe. While it is not uncommon for women to face assault even on the way to school, efforts have been made at a federal level to provide necessary protections, such as the Women's protection bill that recquires thoroughness and evidence in the detention of premarital sex.
