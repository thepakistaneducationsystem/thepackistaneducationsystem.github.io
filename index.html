Education in Pakistan

Goals and Aspirations



Memorization is an important skill to have in Pakistani schools. Lessons often consist of reciting information repeatedly until it is retained.

Religion is a large influence in Pakistani education. A large number of teachers are known to reject the concept of evolution and focus on teaching religious ideas instead.

Education in Pakistan is heavily centered around standard academics. This includes core subjects such as Math, Science, History, and either a course in English or Urdu depending on the region.



Important Statistics

Approximately 44% percent of children aged 5-16 do not attend a form of schooling Pakistan, making it the second highest country in the world in terms of out-of-school children.

At grade nine, only 13% of women are enrolled in school, compared to 35% in men.

The literacy rate for students aged 15-24 is 74%, much higher than the older generations at 46%.

Extracurriculars

In Pakistan, there is a minimal focus on extra-curricular activities due to the ongoing prevalance of academic culture.
